What is Somatic Healing?

In times of trauma and chronic stress, we naturally lose touch with our physical selves. When forced into states of fight, flight, or survival mode, our nervous system becomes dysregulated. Over time, this manifests as shallow breathing, physical tension, and deeply grooved physical holding patterns that we don't know how to break free from.

Somatic healing is the deliberate re-establishment of the connection between the mind and the body. By working with physiological patterns directly, we address somatic dissociation—the feeling of being detached or floating away from yourself—and safely reattach the brain's processing to the body's lived experience.

Answers to Common Questions

How is somatic healing different from traditional psychotherapy?

Traditional psychotherapy heavily emphasizes cognitive processing, verbal narrative, clinical pathology, and diagnostic frameworks. Somatic healing focuses directly on structural bodywork and physiological state regulation. Rather than just talking through an experience, we work directly on the body's physical integration of stress and dissociation, physically reattaching the mind to real-time physical feedback.

Do you accept insurance?

No, insurance is not accepted for somatic healing services. If you are located within the state of California and strictly require formal mental health psychotherapy, you are welcome to reach out to me directly to discuss traditional therapeutic care pathways under my APCC registration.

What should I expect from the initial sessions?

True physiological changes take time; we generally recommend a baseline commitment of at least five sessions to begin shifting deeply grooved somatic holding patterns. Your initial intake session is dedicated to gathering your unique developmental history and physiological background. From there, you will receive tailored somatic recommendations, digital guides, and introductory movement or breathing exercises to begin tracking your nervous system independently.
